Active & Passive Participle

Participles are verb forms used as adjectives to describe nouns. In English, there are two main types of participles: active (present participle) and passive (past participle).

1. Active (Present) Participle

The active participle ends in -ing and describes an action being done by the subject.

- The man driving the car is my uncle.
- We watched the rising sun.

2. Passive (Past) Participle

The passive participle usually ends in -ed, -en, or is an irregular past participle. It describes an action received by the subject.

- The car driven by John was new.
- The broken window was quickly repaired.

Comparison Table

| Type      | Structure                  | Function                 | Example                              |
|-----------|----------------------------|--------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Active    | Verb-ing                   | Describes doing action   | The girl singing is my friend.   |
| Passive   | Past participle (V3)       | Describes receiving action | The song sung by her is beautiful. |

Tips:

  • Active participles describe the one doing the action.
  • Passive participles describe the one receiving the action.
  • Both can be used as adjectives before or after nouns.
