Conditional Sentences

Conditional sentences are used to express actions or events that are dependent on a certain condition. They often contain the word "if".

Types of Conditional Sentences

  • Zero Conditional: Facts or general truths
  • First Conditional: Real and possible situations in the future
  • Second Conditional: Unreal or hypothetical situations in the present or future
  • Third Conditional: Unreal situations in the past

Examples:

- Zero: If you heat water to 100Â°C, it boils.
- First: If it rains tomorrow, we will cancel the trip.
- Second: If I were you, I would study harder.
- Third: If they had left earlier, they would have caught the train.

Conditional Structure Table

| Type   | Condition (If clause)       | Result Clause                   | Example                                          |
|--------|-----------------------------|--------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Zero   | Present Simple              | Present Simple                  | If you mix red and blue, you get purple.         |
| First  | Present Simple              | Will + base verb                | If she calls, I will answer.                     |
| Second | Past Simple                 | Would + base verb               | If I had money, I would travel the world.        |
| Third  | Past Perfect                | Would have + past participle    | If he had studied, he would have passed the exam.|

Tips:

  • Use the correct verb tense for each type of conditional.
  • "If I were..." is correct for hypothetical second conditionals.
  • Third conditionals often express regrets or past possibilities.
