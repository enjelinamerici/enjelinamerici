Relative Pronouns

What Are Relative Pronouns?

Relative pronouns are words that introduce relative clauses. They connect a clause or phrase to a noun or pronoun and provide more information about that noun.

Common Relative Pronouns and Their Uses

  • who – refers to people (used as subject)
  • whom – refers to people (used as object)
  • whose – shows possession (used with people or things)
  • which – refers to animals or things
  • that – refers to people, animals, or things (used in defining clauses)
  • when – refers to time (used to describe a time or moment)
  • what – refers to things or information (used without a clear antecedent)

Usage Rules and Examples

1. Who vs Whom

Who is used as the subject of a clause, while whom is used as the object. 

I know the girl who won the competition. (subject)
The man whom we invited didn't come. (object)

2. Whose (Possession)

Used to show that something belongs to someone. 

The student whose notebook was missing looked upset.
A house whose walls are painted green.

3. Which (Animals/Things)

Refers to animals or objects. Often used in non-defining clauses with commas. 

My car, which I bought last year, is already broken.
The cake which she made was delicious.

4. That (People, Animals, Things - Defining Clause)

Often used instead of "who" or "which" in essential clauses (no commas). 

The phone that rang is mine.
People that eat healthy live longer.

5. When (Time)

Used to refer to a time or moment. 

I remember the day when we first met.
It was a time when people didn’t have mobile phones.

6. What (Things or Information)

Refers to a thing or idea (used independently, not following a noun). 

Tell me what you saw.
She didn’t understand what he meant.

7. Omitting Relative Pronouns

In some cases, the relative pronoun can be omitted, especially when it is the object of the clause. 

The book (that) I read was great.
The boy (whom) you saw is my brother.
