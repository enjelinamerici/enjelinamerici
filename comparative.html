Comparative Adjectives

Comparative adjectives are used to compare two people, animals, things, or places.

1. One-syllable adjectives

For most one-syllable adjectives, add -er at the end.

tall → taller
small → smaller
fast → faster

2. Adjectives ending in "e"

Just add -r.

nice → nicer
large → larger

3. Adjectives ending in consonant + vowel + consonant

Double the last consonant and add -er.

big → bigger
hot → hotter

4. Adjectives with two or more syllables

Use more before the adjective.

beautiful → more beautiful
comfortable → more comfortable

5. Irregular comparatives

good → better
bad → worse
far → farther/further

6. Using “than” in Comparisons

When making comparisons, we usually follow the comparative adjective with than.

- She is taller than her brother.
- This book is more interesting than the last one.

7. Negative Comparisons

We can also use not as...as to show a negative comparison.

- This car is not as fast as my old one.
- He's not as smart as his sister.

8. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don’t use more with adjectives that already take -er. (e.g., ❌ more taller → ✅ taller)
  • Don’t use -er with long adjectives. Use more instead. (e.g., ❌ beautifuller → ✅ more beautiful)

9. Practice Time!

Try writing sentences using these comparative forms:

  • Compare your house to your friend's house.
  • Compare two cities you’ve visited.
  • Compare two things in your classroom.
